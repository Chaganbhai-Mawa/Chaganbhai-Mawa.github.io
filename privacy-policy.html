Privacy Policy

This policy explains how the Live Darshan India mobile app and this website ("the App", "we", "us") handle information. We've written it in plain language so you can see exactly what happens.

The short version: You don't need an account, and we never ask for your name, email or phone number. Your favourites and settings stay on your phone. The App uses two Google services — AdMob to show ads and Firebase for app statistics, crash reports and reminder notifications — which collect device and usage data as described below. Location is optional. Live darshan plays from the temples' official YouTube channels.

1. Who this policy covers

This policy applies to the Live Darshan India app on the Apple App Store and Google Play Store, and to this website. The App is free to use and does not require you to register, sign in, or provide your name, email address or phone number.

2. Information stored on your device

To make the App work, some information is saved locally on your phone. It stays on your phone, we cannot read it, and it is removed when you uninstall the App:

  • Temples you mark as favourites
  • Appearance settings (light/dark mode, accent colour), splash bell sound and auto-scroll preferences
  • Your chosen language
  • Your vrat (fasting) tracker streak
  • Which aarti reminders you've enabled

This information is not tied to your name or identity, and we do not upload it to a server we control.

3. Advertising (Google AdMob)

The App is free and is supported by advertising. Ads are delivered by Google AdMob, a service of Google LLC. To serve and measure ads, AdMob may collect and process:

  • Your device's advertising identifier (Google Advertising ID on Android, IDFA on iOS)
  • IP address, and an approximate location derived from it
  • Device model, operating system version and language
  • Ad interactions, such as which ads were shown, viewed or tapped

This processing is carried out by Google as a third party. Google may use it to show you more relevant advertising, including across other apps and websites that use Google's advertising services. We do not receive your personal information from Google, and we do not build advertising profiles ourselves.

Google describes these practices in its Privacy Policy and its notice on how Google uses information from sites and apps that use its services. We recommend reading both.

Your choices about ads

  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads, where you can delete or reset your advertising ID and turn off ad personalisation.
  • iPhone/iPad: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking. If the App asks for permission to track, choosing "Ask App Not to Track" means the IDFA is not shared for tracking, and the App honours that choice.

Turning off personalisation does not remove ads from the App — it means the ads you see are less tailored to you.

4. Analytics, crash reporting and messaging (Google Firebase)

The App uses Google Firebase, also a service of Google LLC, to understand how the App is used, to fix problems, and to deliver reminder notifications. Depending on the feature, Firebase may collect:

  • Firebase Analytics — an app instance identifier, device model, operating system, language, country or region estimated from IP address, and in-app events such as screens opened, temples viewed and features used. This tells us, for example, that a screen is popular — it does not tell us who you are.
  • Firebase Crashlytics — if the App crashes, a crash report containing the error, the state of the App at the time, device model and operating system version, so we can find and fix the bug.
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging — a device registration token, which is what allows an aarti reminder to reach your phone. The token identifies the installation, not you personally.

We use this information only in aggregate, to improve the App. We do not use it to identify individual users, and we do not sell it or combine it with advertising profiles. Firebase data is processed by Google under Google's Privacy Policy and Firebase's own data processing terms.

You can stop analytics and crash reporting entirely by uninstalling the App. On Android, resetting or deleting your advertising ID also limits how analytics data can be linked to advertising.

5. Location

The App has a "Nearby Darshan" feature that shows temples close to you. This is entirely optional. Location is only accessed if you tap "Use my location" and grant permission through your phone's own permission prompt.

When you do, your approximate location is used on your device to sort temples by distance. We do not store your location, send it to a server of ours, build a location history, or share it with anyone. You can withdraw permission at any time in your phone's settings, and the rest of the App keeps working normally.

Separately, and regardless of this permission, Google may estimate a coarse location from your IP address for advertising and analytics, as described in Sections 3 and 4.

6. Notifications

If you enable aarti reminders, the App sends a notification before an aarti begins at a temple you follow. Notifications require your permission, which your phone will ask for, and can be switched off at any time in the App's notification settings or in your device settings. Delivery uses Firebase Cloud Messaging, as described in Section 4. Reminders are based on the temples and times you choose, not on tracking your behaviour.

7. YouTube and live streams

We do not host, record, re-stream or store any video. Every live darshan is the temple's own official YouTube stream, delivered by YouTube (Google LLC). When a stream plays, YouTube's own terms and privacy policy apply, and YouTube may set cookies or collect viewing data according to its own practices, which are outside our control.

We are not affiliated with, endorsed by, or acting on behalf of any temple trust, and we are not responsible for the availability or content of third-party streams.

8. What we do not do

  • We do not ask you to create an account or give us your name, email or phone number
  • We do not sell or rent your information to anyone
  • We do not access your contacts, photos, camera, microphone or files
  • We do not host or store any video
  • We do not use analytics or crash data to identify you individually

9. Devotional literature

Chalisa, aarti, stotra and mantra texts in the App are in the public domain or made available under an open licence, with sources credited. Reading or listening to them requires no personal information. If you believe any text has been included in error, contact us and we will review and remove it.

10. Permissions summary

  • Internet access — required, to load temple information, streams, ads and notifications
  • Location (approximate or precise) — optional, only for "Nearby Darshan"
  • Notifications — optional, only for aarti reminders

The App does not request camera, microphone, contacts, or file storage permissions. If a future version needs a new permission, we will update this policy before that version is released, and your phone will ask you at the time.

11. Children's privacy

The App is intended for a general audience and is not directed at children under 13 (or the minimum age set by the law where you live). Because the App displays advertising and uses analytics, we ask that children use it with a parent or guardian's involvement. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, contact us and we will delete it.

12. Retention and security

We do not operate accounts, so there is very little for us to hold. Settings and favourites stay on your device and are removed when you uninstall the App. Analytics and crash data are retained by Google for the periods set out in Firebase's documentation and its default retention settings; we do not keep separate copies. If you email us, we keep your message only as long as needed to reply.

Data sent to Google's services is transmitted over encrypted connections. No method of transmission or storage over the internet is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

13. Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or object to the processing of your personal information — for example under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, the EU/UK GDPR, or the California Consumer Privacy Act. In practice:

  • To remove everything stored on your device, uninstall the App
  • To limit advertising data, use the ad settings in Section 3
  • To stop location or notification access, change the permission in your phone settings
  • For anything you've emailed us, write to us and we will delete it on request
  • For data held by Google through AdMob or Firebase, you can also exercise your rights directly with Google using the controls in your Google Account

14. Where your data is processed

Google's advertising and Firebase services process data on servers operated by Google in various countries, which may be outside the country where you live. Google applies its own safeguards for these transfers, described in its Privacy Policy and data processing terms.

15. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy when the App changes or the law requires it. Any material change will be published on this page before it takes effect. We encourage you to check back from time to time.
