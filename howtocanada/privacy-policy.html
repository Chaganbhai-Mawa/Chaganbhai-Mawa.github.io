Privacy Policy

What the HowTo Canada mobile application collects, why it collects it, and the choices you have.

Developer

Chaganbhai Keval Parmar

Privacy contact

Chagan123@yahoo.com

Summary

  • The App has no account, and never asks for your name, email address, phone number or immigration documents.
  • Guides, bookmarks, your province selection, recent searches and display settings stay on your device.
  • Google AdMob provides advertising and Firebase provides analytics and crash reporting; both collect limited technical and usage data described below.
  • The developer does not sell personal information.

Information you provide

None is required. There is no sign-up, no login and no user profile. If you email the developer, you share whatever you put in that message, and it is used only to reply to you.

Information stored only on your device

The following stays in local storage on your phone and is never transmitted to the developer. Deleting the App removes it.

  • Selected province or territory
  • Bookmarked guides
  • Recent search terms
  • Dark mode and other display preferences
  • Values entered into the CRS score estimator, which are calculated on the device

Information collected automatically by third-party services

Google AdMob — advertising

The App shows ads served by Google AdMob. To deliver and measure ads, AdMob may process device and usage information including an advertising identifier (Google Advertising ID on Android, Identifier for Advertisers on iOS), IP address, device model, operating system, coarse location derived from IP address, and interactions with ads. Depending on your settings and region, ads may be personalised or non-personalised.

Google's handling of this data is described in the Google Privacy & Terms and the AdMob documentation.

Google Firebase — analytics and stability

The App uses Firebase services from Google, which may include Google Analytics for Firebase and Firebase Crashlytics. These collect aggregated, event-level information such as app opens, screens viewed, session length, approximate region, device model, operating system version, app version, language, an app-instance identifier, and crash and error diagnostics.

This is used to understand which guides are useful and to fix crashes. It is not used to identify you personally. See Firebase Privacy and Security.

How information is used

  • To display and measure advertising, which keeps the App free
  • To diagnose crashes and technical faults
  • To understand general usage patterns and improve content and features
  • To respond to messages sent to the contact address

The App does not use your information to make decisions about immigration applications, and does not submit anything to any government body.

Sharing

The developer does not sell, rent or trade personal information, and does not share it with advertisers directly. Data is processed by the third-party providers named above acting as service providers, and may be disclosed if required by applicable law or valid legal process.

Your choices and controls

  • Advertising identifier. On Android you can reset or delete your Advertising ID and opt out of ad personalisation in Settings › Google › Ads. On iOS you control app tracking in Settings › Privacy & Security › Tracking; the App requests tracking permission only where required.
  • Consent in the EEA, UK and Switzerland. Where required, the App presents a consent message for personalised advertising, and your choice can be changed or withdrawn from the App's settings.
  • Local data. Clearing the App's storage or uninstalling it removes everything held on the device.
  • Requests. To ask about data associated with your use of the App, or to request deletion of information you sent by email, write to Chagan123@yahoo.com.

Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under laws such as Canada's PIPEDA, the EU and UK GDPR, or the California Consumer Privacy Act — including access, correction, deletion, and objection to certain processing. Requests sent to the contact address are handled within the timeframes set by the applicable law.

Data retention

Information held on your device remains until you delete it or uninstall the App. Analytics, crash and advertising data are retained by Google according to their own retention policies and controls.

Children's privacy

The App is intended for a general adult audience, primarily people navigating Canadian immigration, study, work and settlement processes. It is not directed to children, and the developer does not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information, contact the address above so it can be removed.

Security

Reasonable technical measures protect information, including encrypted transmission to the third-party services named above. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and absolute security cannot be guaranteed.

International transfers

The third-party services named above operate globally, so information they collect may be processed on servers outside your country, including in the United States, under the safeguards those providers describe in their own policies.

Links to other sites

The App links to official government websites such as canada.ca and to provincial resources. Those sites are governed by their own privacy policies, and the developer is not responsible for their content or practices.

Not a government service

HowTo Canada is an independent informational app. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or operated by the Government of Canada or any provincial or territorial government, and it does not provide legal or immigration advice.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated as the App changes or as legal requirements evolve. The current version is always published at this address, and material changes are signalled in the App.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be sent to Chagan123@yahoo.com, or see the contact page.
